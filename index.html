Prehistoric Planet Earth

How its happened

Since its creation, the Earth has undergone changes over the ages. Extinctions, new life, and evolution have all occurred over billions of years.During those times, various animals ruled the earth. During this time, earthquakes, incessant rains, volcanic eruptions, and severe droughts showed from time to time that no one could dominate nature.

Triassic Age

The Triassic Period spanned from 252 to 201 million years ago as the first segment of the Mesozoic Era. Followed by the "Great Dying" mass extinction, it was a pivotal transition time marked by the formation of the supercontinent Pangea and the first appearances of dinosaurs, early mammals, and modern reef-building corals.

Plants

were dominated by hardy, drought-tolerant gymnosperms and spore-producing flora. Because the supercontinent Pangaea was largely hot and arid, plant life consisted mainly of conifers, seed-ferns, cycads, and ferns that could thrive in desert or dry woodland environments.

Animals

The Triassic Period was a critical transitional time following the "Great Dying" extinction. It marked the debut of the first true mammals, the earliest dinosaurs, pterosaurs, and the ancestors of modern reptiles like turtles and crocodiles

Jurassic Age

The Jurassic Period was the middle segment of the Mesozoic Era, lasting from approximately 201.3 million to 145 million years ago. It is most famous as the "golden age of dinosaurs," an era of warm, subtropical climates where massive sauropods and early birds first thrived.

Plants

dominated by non-flowering gymnosperms and spore-producing ferns. Lush forests of towering conifers, cycads, and ginkgoes covered the landscape, providing essential sustenance for massive herbivores. Because they lacked flowers and fruit, these ancient ecosystems were predominantly green, without the vibrant blossoms known today.

Animals

The Jurassic Period (201 to 145 million years ago) was known as the "Age of Giants", marked by the definitive rise of dinosaurs as Earth's dominant land animals. The era is famous for colossal sauropods, large apex predators, diverse marine reptiles, and the very first birds.

The Big Three on that Age

King on the Lands(Tyrannosaurus rex )

It was one of the most powerful apex predators that lived during the Jurassic period. This giant, about 3 m tall and 12 m long, weighed about 9 tons. t rex had the strongest bite force of any known terrestrial animal. Adult T. rex could clamp down with an estimated force of 35,000 to 57,000 Newtons.

King of the Sky(Pterosaur)

if you see the shadow of an airplane under the Jurassic sky, it is the shadow of your death. This giant was the largest flying creature in the Jurassic sky. It was about 16 feet tall, which is about the size of a modern giraffe. When spread out, its wingspan was about 35 feet.

King of the Sea(Shastasaurus )

Shastasaurus was a giant marine reptile measuring roughly 23 to 69 feet (7 to 21 meters) in length and weighing up to 81.5 metric tons. It lacked teeth entirely. Instead of biting, paleontologists believe it used a specialized suction-feeding vacuum method to consume soft-bodied cephalopod.
